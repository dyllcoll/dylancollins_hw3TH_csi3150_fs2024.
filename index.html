What to Wear:

Eastern Market is a vibrant, open-air market, so your outfit should reflect both the season and the casual atmosphere. Here’s what you can wear depending on the time of year:

Spring/Fall: Comfortable layers are key. Consider wearing a light jacket or sweater with jeans or casual pants. Pair with comfortable shoes like sneakers since you’ll likely be walking around a lot.

Summer: Opt for light, breathable fabrics like cotton. A t-shirt, shorts, or a casual summer dress would work well. Don’t forget sunglasses and sunscreen, as parts of the market can be in direct sunlight.

Winter: Detroit winters can be cold, so bundle up! A warm coat, gloves, scarf, and a hat are a must. Wear sturdy, insulated boots if there’s snow on the ground.

Regardless of the season, since you’ll be navigating through outdoor stalls and market spaces, comfortable shoes are a must.

Reservations:

While Eastern Market is mostly a walk-in experience with vendors and food stalls, if you’re planning to dine at one of the nearby restaurants or cafes, making a reservation is a good idea, especially during weekends or event days. Popular spots around Eastern Market include:

  • Supino Pizzeria: Famous for its delicious, thin-crust pizza.
  • Detroit City Distillery: Great for cocktails and small bites.
  • Vivio’s: Known for its iconic Bloody Marys and casual dining.
